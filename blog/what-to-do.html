What do Hackers Do

When hackers hack your account, they want to get some benefits. One major motivation is money. Hackers might sell you information to earn money. They could delete or alter your information. Or, they could use your account to pretend to be you and use this information to scam or hack other people that you are in contact with. Worse of all, they could change your password and prevent you from accessing your account.

Being hacked may result in identity theft, data loss, being spied on by malicious or infected software, frozen accounts, or inability to access your account. This is more serious when it is a work account, which will result in consequences such as revenue loss, reputation damage, unhappy customers, inability to do your job or pay your employees, and loss and leaking of sensitive information.

What to Do

If you are hacked, be sure to inform the company your account is associated with. If it is a social media account, be sure to tell your contacts that you have been hacked, and messages sent may not be from you. Be sure to inform your friends and family.

Next, log in to your account and immediately change your password so the hacker can no longer access it. Remember to use a secure password, or the hacker might hack you again. Make sure your new password is completely different from your old one. Then, secure your login process. For example, enable two-factor authentication. Then, check for suspicious logins, see if your other accounts have been hacked, unlink online accounts to minimize damage, update your software and operating system, start using a second email address as backup, or turn off location settings on apps.

If it is serious, scan your devices for malware with a trusted source and set up identity theft protection and monitoring. You can also file a report with the Federal Trade Commission. If you need any help in restoring your account, contact the company.
